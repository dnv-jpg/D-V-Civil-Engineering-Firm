Assignment 0

Video reflections and company setup. IE492 Spring 2026, Dr. Ranky.

Video 1: Bentley Quality Design

Bentley design and engineering software for infrastructure and construction; design, modeling, quality assurance.

Design quality, consistency across teams, integrating engineering data with delivery, reducing rework.

Integrated design platforms, digital workflows, quality focused processes.

Quality belongs in the design process; integrated systems help align scope, schedule, and quality.

Video 2: Chemical Engineering Process (Siemens)

Siemens process automation; industrial process control, safety, continuous improvement.

Complexity, safety, compliance, throughput and quality in an integrated environment.

Automation, process control, monitoring, validation, human and machine coordination.

No system is isolated; process projects need clear requirements, risk management, and technology plus training.

Video 3: Cognex Vision Inspection

Cognex machine vision for automated inspection; image capture, analysis, pass/fail on the line.

Fast, reliable, accurate inspection with product variation and integration with production and quality.

Vision hardware and software, calibration and validation, integration with PLCs and quality data.

Clear acceptance criteria, validation plans, and alignment with production and quality so data supports decisions.

Video 4: FANUC Fixtureless Robotic Assembly

FANUC robotic assembly without fixtures; vision and force feedback for flexible part handling and changeover.

Replacing fixed fixtures with flexible automation while keeping cycle time, quality, and reliability.

Vision guided and force controlled robotics, flexible tooling, structured programming and validation.

Flexibility vs standardization is a trade off; clear requirements and phased validation help automation projects.

Video 5: FLOW Waterjet Cutting

FLOW waterjet cutting; high pressure water (and abrasives) for precision cutting with minimal thermal impact.

Consistent cut quality, consumables and maintenance, integration with production and quality.

Process parameter control, maintenance and calibration, integration with CAD/CAM and shop floor management.

Equipment projects need a lifecycle view: procurement, installation, training, maintenance, and metrics.

Video 6: Ford Rouge Body

Ford Rouge body shop; body in white assembly, automation, multiple lines in a large plant.

Coordinating production, quality, and improvement across many stations and suppliers; safety, cost, delivery.

Lean and visual management, standardized work, automation where it adds value, safety and quality gates.

Large projects need governance, stakeholder alignment, and integration of people, process, and technology.
